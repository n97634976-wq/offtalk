When cellular networks fail or Wi-Fi is unavailable, OffTalk shines.\n\nBy leveraging severe **Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE)** mesh networking, OffTalk turns every user\'s phone into a decentralized router. Messages hop securely from device to device until they reach their intended recipient, allowing communication over vast distances without a single cell tower or central server.\n\n## ✨ Key Features\n\n- 📡 **True Peer-to-Peer Mesh Network**: Messages route dynamically through nearby devices via a local Kademlia DHT algorithm. No internet required. Ever.\n- 🔐 **Military-Grade E2E Encryption**: Every message is protected by PointyCastle AES-GCM encryption. Not even the relay nodes forwarding your message can read it.\n- 🪪 **Offline Identity Verification**: Add contacts securely offline using QR codes or NFC Bumping. We use hardware SIM features (ICCID/IMSI) to verify identity without phone numbers or SMS.\n- 🗺️ **Offline High-Res Maps**: Download local map tiles cache via `flutter_map_tile_caching` to view the geographic positions of peers without Google Maps.\n- 😎 **Expressive Chat**: Send secure text messages, self-destructing secure messages (TTL), and use the built-in offline **Emoji Keyboard** to express yourself securely.\n- ⏹️ **Hardware Security Lockout**: Your local SQLCipher database is encrypted on-device. Enter the wrong PIN 3 times, and the app locks out to prevent brute-force attacks.\n\n---\n\n## 📲 How To Download & Install\n\nSince this app is not on the Google Play Store (to maintain maximum censorship resistance), you must install it directly via the APK.\n\n### Option 1: Direct Download (From GitHub)\n1. Navigate to the **[Releases](../../releases)** page of this GitHub repository.\n2. Download the latest `app-release.apk` file to your Android phone.\n3. Open the file manager on your phone and tap the downloaded APK.\n4. When prompted, tap **"Settings"** and allow **"Install from unknown sources"**.\n5. Click **Install**.\n\n### Option 2: Receive from a Friend (Via WhatsApp / Bluetooth)\nIf a friend already has the APK file or the installed app:\n1. Have them send the `.apk` file directly to you over WhatsApp, Telegram, or Nearby Share.\n2. Tap the file in your chat and press **Install**.\n\n---\n\n## 🧭 How To Use OffTalk\n\n### 1. Setup Your Secure PIN\nWhen you first launch the app, you will be prompted to create a 6-digit PIN. \n> ⚠️ **DO NOT LOSE THIS PIN**. OffTalk has absolutely zero central servers. If you forget your PIN, there is no "Forgot Password" button. Your messages will be permanently lost.\n\n### 2. Pairing with Contacts (Must be done in person!)\nBecause there is no central server to look up usernames, you must exchange cryptographic keys with your friends in person before you can chat.\n1. Go to the **Chats** tab and tap the circular "+" button in the bottom right.\n2. Have your friend open the **Profile** tab on their phone.\n3. **QR Code**: Scan their QR code with your camera.\n4. **NFC Bump**: Alternatively, tap the top of your phones together to instantly pair!\n\n### 3. Chatting & Expressing via Emojis\nOnce paired, tap their name in the Chat list. Type your message, use the newly integrated **Emoji Button** (😊) to access hundreds of offline emojis, and hit send! The message will encrypt, bounce through nearby Bluetooth devices, and arrive securely.\n\n### 4. Viewing the Mesh Network\nCurious who is relaying your messages? Tap the **Mesh** tab at the bottom to see a live monitor of active Bluetooth peers, your active routing table, and any messages waiting in your packet queue.\n\n---\n\n## 🛠️ How It Works (Under the Hood)\n\nOffTalk utilizes a combination of advanced local-first technologies:\n- **Flutter & Riverpod**: For a blazingly fast, reactive user interface across platforms.\n- **flutter_blue_plus**: For managing low-level Bluetooth LE connections and GATT characteristics to broadcast encrypted payload packets.\n- **Hive & SQLCipher**: For dual-layer database storage. Fast NoSQL for the routing registry, and encrypted SQL for private chat histories.\n- **dart_libp2p**: Utilizes Kademlia Distributed Hash Tables (DHT) to discover the shortest Bluetooth routing path between two nodes.\n\n---\n\n## 💻 Developer Guide: How to Contribute & Commit Changes\n\nIf you want to modify the code and push your changes to GitHub, follow these exact steps:\n\n### Building the App Locally\n1.
